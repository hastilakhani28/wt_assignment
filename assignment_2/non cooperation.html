NON COOPERATION MOVEMENT(1920-1922)

The Non-Cooperation Movement (1920-1922), led by Mahatma Gandhi, was a peaceful protest against British rule in India. It involved boycotting British goods, institutions, and honors, promoting self-reliance through indigenous products, and striving for self-governance. The movement was suspended after the Chauri Chaura incident in 1922.
